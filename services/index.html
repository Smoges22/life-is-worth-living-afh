02

Complete Care Guide

Detailed support for daily living, advanced needs, and family peace of mind.

Families often come to us with practical questions: What care is included? Can you support changing needs? How will we know our loved one is safe and comfortable? These sections explain the care experience in plain language.

01

Care Overview

Life Worth Living AFH provides personalized support in a smaller residential setting, guided by healthcare experience and a deep respect for each resident's dignity.

Right fit if your family wants attentive care, clear communication, and a calmer home environment.

02

Daily Living Support

We assist with bathing, dressing, grooming, toileting, mobility, transfers, meals, hydration, and daily routines in a respectful, patient way.

Helpful for residents who need hands-on support but still deserve independence and choice.

03

RN-Led Clinical Oversight

RN-led leadership helps us notice changes, communicate concerns, support care plans, and coordinate with families, providers, hospice, or home health when appropriate.

Ideal when families want a clinical eye in a warm home setting.

04

Dementia & Memory Care

Residents with memory changes benefit from structure, calm routines, redirection, familiarity, safety awareness, and meaningful daily engagement.

A good fit for residents who need supervision, patience, and reassurance throughout the day.

05

Medication & Chronic Condition Management

We support medication routines, monitor day-to-day changes, and help families navigate chronic conditions such as diabetes, mobility changes, and complex care needs.

Helpful when missed medications or changing health needs have become stressful at home.

06

End-of-Life & Hospice Support

We provide compassionate comfort-focused care, dignity, family communication, and collaboration with hospice providers during sensitive stages of life.

Right fit when comfort, respect, and family-centered communication matter most.

07

Rehabilitation & Post-Hospital Recovery

After a hospital or rehabilitation stay, residents may need help rebuilding routines, staying safe, following instructions, and coordinating follow-up care.

Useful for families worried about falls, weakness, medication changes, or readmission risk.

08

Wound Care & Advanced Nursing Support

RN-informed oversight may support wound care coordination, wound-VAC management, diabetes support, urological care, gastrointestinal care, and careful observation.

We review needs individually to confirm whether the home is appropriate.

09

Nutrition, Meals & Daily Routines

Residents receive nutritious meals, hydration support, household routines, laundry, housekeeping, and encouragement around comfortable daily rhythms.

Helpful for loved ones who need consistency, reminders, or support eating well.

10

Family Communication & Care Coordination

Families can expect practical updates, respectful communication, and care coordination when needs change or outside providers are involved.

Especially valuable when family members are coordinating care from different households or cities.

11

Lifestyle, Activities & Spiritual Support

We support meaningful routines, family visits, conversation, music, simple activities, outdoor time, and faith-based support when desired by the resident.

Care should support the whole person, not only the task list.
